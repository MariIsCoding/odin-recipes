French Fries

Description

Learn how to make McDonald’s classic French Fry recipe at home! (And exactly how the restaurant does it!) These fries are golden and crispy on the outside, and soft on the inside!



Ingredients

  • Canola Oil
  • Potatoes
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Soybean Oil
  • Hydrogenated Soybean Oil
  • Beef Flavor
  • Hydrolyzed Wheat
  • Hydrolyzed Milk
  • Citric Acid
  • Dimethylpolysiloxane
  • Dextrose
  • Sodium Acid Pyrophoaphate
  • Cornstarch
  • Corn Oil
  • TBHQ

Steps

  1. Create a brine using a combination of cold water, corn syrup, salt, and white vinegar. Slice the potatoes into strings just under 1/2 inch thick. Drop into the brine.
  2. Cover and refrigerate for 2 hours or as long as overnight. Drain, rise twice with cold water, then pat them completely dry.
  3. Fry in batches at 300°F for about 5 minutes. Remove and set aside on a paper towel. They will be very soft. (This is a good thing.)
  4. Add vegetable shortening to the oil. Increase heat to 400°F and fry again until the fries are crisp and golden brown. Remove and let drain on paper towels. Season immediately with salt and serve!
